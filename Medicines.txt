Panadol
Panadol is an effective analgesic and antipyretic. It works by blocking chemical transporters in the brain to control pain and regulate body temperature.

Indications for the use of Panadol include:
Colds and flu, Muscle and joint pain, Postoperative pain, Menstrual pain, Migraine, Toothache, Sore throat, Fever.

Dosage:
Adults and children over 12: One or two tablets every 4–6 hours.
Each dose should be separated by at least 4 hours.
Do not exceed the maximum daily dose.

Side Effects:
Abdominal pain, Constipation, Headaches, Nausea.

Warnings:
Consult a doctor if symptoms persist beyond 3 days.
Paracetamol is generally safe when taken at the correct dose.

Augmentin
Augmentin is a broad-spectrum antibiotic that contains amoxicillin and clavulanic acid. It works by preventing bacterial cell wall formation and inhibiting beta-lactamase enzymes.

Indications for the use of Augmentin include:
Respiratory tract infections, Ear infections, Urinary tract infections, Skin and soft tissue infections, Dental infections, Bone and joint infections.

Dosage:
Adults and children over 12: 500 mg/125 mg every 8 hours or 875 mg/125 mg every 12 hours, depending on infection severity.
Take with food to reduce gastrointestinal discomfort.
Treatment lasts 5 to 14 days.
Children’s dose is weight-based and prescribed by a doctor.

Side Effects:
Diarrhea, Nausea, Vomiting, Abdominal pain, Rash, Headache, Yeast infections.

Warnings:
Complete the full course even if symptoms improve early.
Consult a doctor if severe diarrhea or rash develops.

Voltaren
Voltaren is a non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID) that contains diclofenac sodium or potassium. It works by inhibiting COX enzymes, reducing prostaglandin production.

Indications for the use of Voltaren include:
Muscle and joint pain, Back pain, Osteoarthritis, Rheumatoid arthritis, Ankylosing spondylitis, Menstrual pain, Postoperative pain, Migraine, Tendonitis, Bursitis.

Dosage:
Tablets: 50 mg taken 2–3 times daily. Maximum 150 mg/day.
Gel: Apply a thin layer to affected area 3–4 times daily.
Injection: Used under medical supervision for severe pain.
Take oral form with food.

Side Effects:
Stomach pain, Indigestion, Nausea, Headache, Dizziness, Rash or itching, Elevated liver enzymes.

Warnings:
Do not exceed recommended dose or apply gel to broken skin.
Avoid long-term use without medical advice.

Nexium
Nexium is a proton pump inhibitor (PPI) that contains esomeprazole. It works by reducing stomach acid production through inhibition of the gastric proton pump.

Indications for the use of Nexium include:
GERD, Stomach ulcers, Erosive esophagitis, Zollinger-Ellison syndrome, H. pylori eradication, Prevention of gastric ulcers.

Dosage:
GERD: 20–40 mg once daily for 4–8 weeks.
Ulcers: 20 mg once daily.
H. pylori: 20 mg twice daily with antibiotics.
Take at least 1 hour before meals. Do not crush or chew tablets.

Side Effects:
Headache, Nausea, Abdominal pain, Constipation, Flatulence, Diarrhea, Dry mouth.

Warnings:
Long-term use may cause vitamin B12 deficiency, low magnesium, or increased fracture risk.
May mask gastric cancer symptoms.
Consult a doctor if no improvement after 14 days or if severe symptoms occur.

Advil
Advil is a non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID) that contains ibuprofen. It works by inhibiting the cyclooxygenase (COX) enzymes, reducing the production of prostaglandins, which helps relieve pain, inflammation, and fever.

Indications for the use of Advil include:
Headache, Toothache, Menstrual cramps, Back pain, Muscle aches, Arthritis, Minor injuries, Fever, Common cold symptoms.

Dosage:
Adults and children over 12: 200–400 mg every 4–6 hours as needed.
Do not exceed 1200 mg per day unless directed by a doctor.
Should be taken with food or milk to reduce stomach irritation.
Children’s dosage is weight-based and must be prescribed.

Side Effects:
Nausea, Heartburn, Dizziness, Headache, Stomach pain, Constipation, Diarrhea.

Warnings:
Long-term use or high doses can increase risk of gastrointestinal bleeding, ulcers, kidney damage, and heart problems.
Avoid if allergic to NSAIDs or if you have a history of ulcers or bleeding disorders.
Consult a doctor if symptoms persist more than a few days or worsen.

Flagyl
Flagyl is an antibiotic and antiprotozoal medication that contains metronidazole. It works by disrupting the DNA synthesis of bacteria and protozoa, leading to cell death.

Indications for the use of Flagyl include:
Bacterial vaginosis, Trichomoniasis, Giardiasis, Amoebiasis, Pelvic inflammatory disease (PID), Intra-abdominal infections, Clostridium difficile-associated diarrhea, Dental infections, Anaerobic bacterial infections.

Dosage:
Adults: 500–750 mg orally every 8 hours, depending on infection type and severity.
For trichomoniasis or bacterial vaginosis: A single dose of 2 g or 500 mg twice daily for 7 days.
Children’s dosage is weight-based and must be prescribed.
Take with food to reduce stomach upset. Avoid alcohol during treatment and for at least 48 hours after the last dose.

Side Effects:
Nausea, Metallic taste, Headache, Dizziness, Loss of appetite, Abdominal cramps, Diarrhea, Darkened urine.

Warnings:
Avoid alcohol — may cause a serious reaction (flushing, vomiting, rapid heartbeat).
Use with caution in patients with liver disease or blood disorders.
Prolonged use may lead to fungal overgrowth or nerve toxicity.
Consult a doctor if neurological symptoms or allergic reactions occur.

Otrivin
Otrivin is a nasal decongestant that contains xylometazoline hydrochloride. It works by constricting the blood vessels in the nasal passages, leading to reduced swelling and easier breathing.

Indications for the use of Otrivin include:
Nasal congestion due to common cold, Sinusitis, Allergic rhinitis, Hay fever, Upper respiratory tract infections.

Dosage:
Adults and children over 12: 1 spray into each nostril every 8–10 hours as needed (max 3 times/day).
Children 6–12: Use lower-strength formulation under adult supervision.
Do not use for more than 5–7 consecutive days to avoid rebound congestion.
Blow nose gently before use.

Side Effects:
Temporary nasal burning or stinging, Dryness in nose or throat, Sneezing, Headache, Nausea, Restlessness (in sensitive individuals).

Warnings:
Do not use for more than 7 days continuously — may cause rebound congestion (rhinitis medicamentosa).
Not recommended for children under 6 unless prescribed.
Avoid use in people with heart disease, high blood pressure, thyroid disorders, or diabetes without medical advice.
Consult a doctor if symptoms persist or worsen.

Amoxicillin
Amoxicillin is a broad-spectrum penicillin-type antibiotic. It works by inhibiting the synthesis of bacterial cell walls, leading to the death of sensitive bacteria.

Indications for the use of Amoxicillin include:
Respiratory tract infections, Ear infections (otitis media), Urinary tract infections (UTIs), Skin and soft tissue infections, Dental infections, Sinusitis, Tonsillitis, Helicobacter pylori infection (part of combination therapy).

Dosage:
Adults: 250–500 mg every 8 hours or 500–875 mg every 12 hours, depending on the type and severity of the infection.
Children: Dosage is weight-based, typically 20–45 mg/kg/day divided into doses.
Can be taken with or without food.
Complete the full course as prescribed, even if symptoms improve early.

Side Effects:
Nausea, Diarrhea, Abdominal pain, Rash, Headache, Vomiting, Yeast infections (oral/vaginal).

Warnings:
Not suitable for patients allergic to penicillin or beta-lactam antibiotics.
May cause allergic reactions, including rash, itching, or anaphylaxis.
Use with caution in patients with kidney impairment.
Seek medical attention if severe diarrhea or rash occurs during treatment.

Prednisolone
Description:
Prednisolone is a corticosteroid with potent anti-inflammatory and immunosuppressive effects.

Indications:
Asthma, allergies, autoimmune diseases, inflammation.

Dosage:
5–60 mg daily depending on condition.

Side Effects:
Weight gain, fluid retention, mood swings, osteoporosis.

Warnings:
Do not stop abruptly. Use lowest effective dose. Monitor blood sugar and bone health.

Tavanic
Description:
Tavanic (levofloxacin) is a broad-spectrum fluoroquinolone antibiotic.

Indications:
Respiratory infections, UTIs, sinusitis, skin infections.

Dosage:
250–750 mg once daily.

Side Effects:
Nausea, diarrhea, tendonitis, dizziness.

Warnings:
Avoid in children and pregnancy. Risk of tendon rupture and QT prolongation.

Buscopan
Description:
Buscopan (hyoscine butylbromide) is an antispasmodic used to relieve smooth muscle cramps.

Indications:
Abdominal cramps, IBS, biliary or urinary colic.

Dosage:
10–20 mg up to 4 times daily.

Side Effects:
Dry mouth, dizziness, blurred vision, tachycardia.

Warnings:
Avoid in glaucoma, myasthenia gravis, and prostate hypertrophy.

Erythrocin
Description:
Erythrocin (erythromycin) is a macrolide antibiotic used to treat a variety of bacterial infections.

Indications:
Respiratory infections, skin infections, STDs, pertussis.

Dosage:
250–500 mg every 6 hours.

Side Effects:
GI upset, liver enzyme changes, QT prolongation.

Warnings:
Use caution in liver disease. Avoid with certain cardiac meds due to QT risk.

Zomig
Description:
Zomig (zolmitriptan) is a selective serotonin receptor agonist for acute migraine treatment.

Indications:
Migraine attacks with or without aura.

Dosage:
2.5 mg once, may repeat after 2 hours if needed. Max 10 mg/day.

Side Effects:
Tight chest, dizziness, tingling, fatigue.

Warnings:
Not for prevention. Avoid in heart disease. Do not exceed max dose.


Rivotril
Description:
Rivotril (clonazepam) is a benzodiazepine used to treat seizures and panic disorders.

Indications:
Epilepsy, panic disorder, anxiety.

Dosage:
0.5–1 mg once or twice daily, adjust as needed.

Side Effects:
Drowsiness, coordination issues, dependence, respiratory depression.

Warnings:
Taper slowly. Avoid alcohol. Risk of addiction and withdrawal seizures.

Tegretol
Description:
Tegretol (carbamazepine) is an anticonvulsant and mood stabilizer.

Indications:
Epilepsy, trigeminal neuralgia, bipolar disorder.

Dosage:
200–400 mg/day, up to 1200 mg/day in divided doses.

Side Effects:
Dizziness, nausea, blurred vision, rash.

Warnings:
Monitor CBC and liver enzymes. Risk of serious skin reactions (e.g., Stevens-Johnson syndrome).

Motrin
Description:
Motrin (ibuprofen) is an NSAID used for pain relief, fever, and inflammation.

Indications:
Pain, fever, arthritis, dysmenorrhea.

Dosage:
200–400 mg every 4–6 hours. Max 2400 mg/day.

Side Effects:
GI upset, ulcers, kidney issues, dizziness.

Warnings:
Avoid in ulcers or renal impairment. Use lowest effective dose.
